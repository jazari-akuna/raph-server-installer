storage volumes

Two encrypted volumes are provisioned at finalize time:

  • personal — one per user, on a sparse LUKS-on-loopback blob under /srv/store/data/<user>.img. The same size applies to every user created post-setup (you can override per-user later by editing the source).
  • shared — one system-wide blob mounted at /srv/store/mnt/_shared, exposed by copyparty as /shared. Unlocked at boot by a host keyfile, not a passphrase — convenient at the cost of weaker at-rest confidentiality (see create-shared-volume.sh's trust- model footnote).

Both volumes are sparse: they don't allocate disk up-front, only as data is written. Suggested defaults below come from the disk's free space: {{prettyBytes .DiskFreeBytes}} free of {{prettyBytes .DiskTotalBytes}} total under /srv/store.

You can set values larger than the free disk — the volumes are sparse, so the only consequence is the operator promising future capacity that doesn't exist yet. The wizard does not argue with explicit choices.
